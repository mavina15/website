Aquaponics

At the moment, I am studying aquaponics and its potency on the health of working class communities of color in the Los Angeles area. For example the cities of Lennox, Inglewood, and Hawthorne have limited options when it comes to healthy and fresh food; there are barriers for the residents of these neighborhoods, like cost, that make it more difficult and less likely for people to adopt a healthy lifestyle. Aquaponics can bring community members, more specifically children, to be exposed to sustainably growing edible plants and edible fish while also being exposed to technology with the use of automation. This project covers essential ideas in biology, chemistry, math, physics, and computer science. By introducing students to STEM, they have the opportunity to develop critical thinking and problem solving skills early on, which can give them a competitive advantage later on in life.

Math Education

As a senior, I intend to apply to education programs in pursuit of Math teaching credentials employing a social justice and equity philosophy. As an educator, my goal is to expose youth of color to STEM in a way that builds them up to be agents of change in the present and future, if we are lucky to be guaranteed one. I want to help underrepresented students from Los Angeles bridge concepts in Algebra and Calculus to their everyday life, as it is where the educational system excludes so many students.
